Here the best movies ever

Amelie

A story about a girl named Amélie whose childhood was suppressed by her father's mistaken concerns of a heart defect. With these concerns Amélie gets hardly any real-life contact with other people. This leads her to resort to her own fantastical world and dreams of love and beauty.

Dead Poets Socaity

Dead Poets Society is a 1989 American coming-of-age drama film directed by Peter Weir and written by Tom Schulman. The film is set in 1959 at a fictional elite boarding school called Welton Academy, and tells the story of an English teacher who inspires his students through his teaching of poetry. The teacher, Mr. Keating, also encourages the re-formation of the secret group "Dead Poets Society," where students gather to read and experience poetry together.

Coraline

Coraline is a 2009 American gothic stop-motion animated dark fantasy horror film written for the screen and directed by Henry Selick, based on the 2002 novella of the same name by Neil Gaiman. Produced by Laika

The Father

The Father is a 2020 psychological drama film, directed by Florian Zeller in his directorial debut. He co-wrote the screenplay with fellow playwright Christopher Hampton on the basis of Zeller's 2012 play Le Père.

Inception

Inception is a 2010 science fiction action heist film written and directed by Christopher Nolan, who also produced it with Emma Thomas, his wife. The film stars Leonardo DiCaprio as a professional thief who steals information by infiltrating the subconscious of his targets.

The Perks Of Being A Wallflower

"The Perks of Being a Wallflower" is a heartfelt coming-of-age movie based on a best-selling novel. It explores themes of friendship, self-discovery, and the challenges of youth in the early 1990s1. The film follows 15-year-old Charlie, an endearing and naive outsider, as he copes with first love, the suicide of his best friend, and his own mental illness while struggling to find a group of people with whom he belongs
